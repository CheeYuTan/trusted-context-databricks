## Closing Thoughts

Part 4 is about making calculation rules explicit.

The important questions are:

```text
Percent of what?
Over what time frame?
Built from which measures?
Understood with which business language?
```

Metric Views let those answers live in the governed semantic layer.

Part 2 introduced the KPI contract. Part 3 showed how to model relationships. Part 4 showed how to make the calculations richer and more reusable.

The remaining production question is performance:

> How do trusted Metric Views stay fast when dashboards, SQL users, BI tools, Genie Spaces, and agents all depend on them?

That is where materialization fits.
